What to Expect During Your Consultation

Your first step toward resolving your family law matter

Initial Consultation Process

During your initial consultation, Attorney Miller will:

  • Listen carefully to your situation and concerns
  • Ask questions to understand your legal needs
  • Explain relevant laws and how they apply to your case
  • Discuss potential legal strategies and options
  • Outline what to expect during the legal process
  • Address any questions you may have

What to Bring

To make the most of your consultation, please bring:

  • Any court documents related to your case
  • Relevant agreements (prenuptial, marital, etc.)
  • Financial information if relevant to your matter
  • Timeline of important events related to your case
  • List of questions or concerns you want to address
  • Information about other involved parties

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your fee structure?

We offer various fee structures depending on your case type and needs. These include hourly rates, flat fees for certain services, and limited-scope representation options. During your initial consultation, we'll discuss which approach makes the most sense for your specific situation.

 How long will my case take?

Each family law case is unique, and timelines can vary significantly based on complexity, court schedules, and the level of agreement between parties. Simple uncontested matters may be resolved in a few months, while more complex contested cases could take a year or longer. We strive to resolve all matters as efficiently as possible while protecting your interests.

 Do you offer virtual consultations?

Yes, we offer consultations by phone or video conference for clients who prefer remote meetings. These virtual options provide the same level of confidentiality and personal attention as in-person meetings.
